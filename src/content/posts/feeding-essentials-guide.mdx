Walk into any baby store and the feeding section will make your head spin. Bottle warmers, formula mixers, self-sterilizing everything. Here's what you *actually* need.

## The Bottles

Every baby is different, so buy **one** pack of a brand and test before committing. We went through three brands before finding the one our baby would accept.

<BudgetCallout>
Don't buy a full bottle set until your baby picks a favorite. Most stores accept returns on unopened packs. Start with a 2-pack.
</BudgetCallout>

## The High Chair

You'll use this for *years*, so it's worth investing in one that:
- Wipes clean easily (fabric seats are a trap)
- Has a removable tray that fits in the dishwasher
- Grows with your child

## Bibs That Actually Catch Food

Silicone bibs with a pocket. That's it. Cloth bibs are for drooling, not for eating. The pocket catches about 80% of what would otherwise end up on the floor.

## What We Returned

- **Bottle warmer** — hot water in a mug works just as well
- **Formula mixing pitcher** — nice in theory, annoying to clean
- **Baby food maker** — a regular steamer and blender do the same thing

<SafetyNote>
When starting solids, always supervise your baby. Learn the difference between **gagging** (normal, loud, baby handles it) and **choking** (silent, requires intervention). Take an infant CPR class before starting solids.
</SafetyNote>

## Our Starting Solids Timeline

We followed a baby-led weaning approach starting at 6 months. Here's roughly what that looked like:

1. **6 months** — soft steamed veggies, banana, avocado
2. **7-8 months** — adding proteins, yogurt, toast strips
3. **9-10 months** — family meals cut into appropriate sizes
4. **12 months** — eating almost everything we eat

---

**The truth:** You need far less gear than the registry checklists suggest. Start simple, add as needed, and don't let anyone make you feel like you need a $200 bottle system.
