## What to Know Before You Buy

No recalls on either carrier. Both are safe when used correctly and both keep babies in the hip-healthy frog-leg M-position, which meets the hip dysplasia prevention standard from pediatric physiotherapy organizations.

The Solly Baby Wrap has a weight limit of 25 pounds. In practice it is a 0-to-4-months carrier for most babies. It is not designed for back carries, and some moms find the wrap loses its snug feel as babies get heavier and more active. That is not a safety issue. It does shorten the useful runway.

One detail on the Ergobaby Omni 360: for newborns, some versions ship with an infant insert and some do not. Check the specific listing you are buying. Listings on Amazon vary. The infant insert is what makes the seat narrow enough for babies under roughly 12 pounds.

## Will It Last?

The Ergobaby Omni 360 is a genuine long-haul carrier. At 7 to 45 pounds it covers the full baby and toddler window. Families use it for travel, grocery runs, hikes, and any situation where a stroller is not practical. The build quality is consistently praised at the 12- and 18-month mark in reviews.

The Solly Baby Wrap is a phase product. It is exceptional for the newborn phase through early infancy. Most moms phase it out between three and five months. If you buy only the Solly, plan on needing a structured carrier within a few months. If you buy only the Ergobaby, the newborn setup curve is steeper than a wrap but manageable with YouTube.

The smartest setup for many families: Solly for the first few months, Ergobaby Omni 360 for everything after. If budget allows for only one, get the Ergobaby and watch a wrap tutorial before the baby comes.
