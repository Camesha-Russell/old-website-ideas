If there's one thing every new parent loses, it's sleep. We've tested *dozens* of products over the past two years — most of them ended up in the donation pile. These five actually stayed.

## 1. The Crib That Made Us Believers

We were skeptical of spending more on a crib, but the difference in build quality was night and day. Look for solid wood construction, JPMA certification, and a mattress that fits without gaps.

<SafetyNote>
Always follow the ABCs of safe sleep: **Alone**, on their **Back**, in a **Crib** with nothing else inside. No blankets, no bumpers, no stuffed animals.
</SafetyNote>

## 2. A Sound Machine That Actually Works

Forget the ones with 47 nature sounds. You need one thing: consistent white noise at a safe decibel level. We measured ours at 50 dB from the crib — right in the recommended range.

## 3. The Swaddle Transition Suit

Around 3-4 months when the swaddle has to go, this transitional suit was a lifesaver. Arms-up design, gentle compression, and our baby barely noticed the change.

<BudgetCallout>
**Budget pick:** A simple muslin sleep sack works nearly as well for half the price. The fancy ones just have better zippers.
</BudgetCallout>

## 4. Blackout Curtains (Non-Negotiable)

We tried three different brands before finding ones that actually block *all* light. The key: look for curtains with a white backing that velcro to the window frame, not just the rod.

## 5. A Simple Baby Monitor

Skip the smart monitors with oxygen tracking and sleep analytics. A basic video monitor with night vision and two-way audio is all you need — and it won't give you anxiety every time it sends a notification.

---

**Bottom line:** Good sleep products don't need to be expensive. They need to be *safe*, *simple*, and *actually tested* by someone who isn't being paid to say they love them.
