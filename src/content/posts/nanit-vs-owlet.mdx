## What to Know Before You Buy

The Owlet Dream Sock is FDA-cleared as a consumer wellness device. It is not cleared as a diagnostic medical device. For healthy term babies it provides real-time peace of mind. For babies with confirmed health concerns, ask your pediatrician what monitoring level is appropriate for your specific situation.

One honest flag on Owlet: some parents report false alarms. An alert for low oxygen that resolves when the sock is adjusted. The most common cause is the sock slipping on a moving baby. It is not a safety risk. It does mean a 2am alert that sends your heart racing and turns out to be a fit issue rather than a health one.

Both monitors are Wi-Fi dependent. If your home Wi-Fi is spotty near the nursery, factor that in before spending $299 to $399 on either.

## Will It Last?

Both the Nanit and Owlet are designed for the first two years. The Owlet sock comes in three sizes so it grows with the baby. The Nanit camera stays mounted and useful for as long as you want video monitoring.

One practical note: both require ongoing subscriptions for their premium features. Nanit Insights is $100 per year. Owlet Care+ is $120 per year. Over two years, that is an additional $200 to $240 on top of the hardware. Build that into your budget calculation before you buy either one.

The Infant Optics DXR-8 has no subscription, ever. What you pay at purchase is what you pay total. Over two years, that is a real difference.
