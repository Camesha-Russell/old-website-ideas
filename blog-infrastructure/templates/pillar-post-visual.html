The Boba Wrap doesn't do everything the Ergobaby does. It's a stretch wrap — one piece of fabric, no buckles, no hardware. What to Look For

⑩ Buying criteria: specific to THIS category, not generic tips. These should be the exact factors that informed the picks above — so the post's reasoning is transparent. If Maya reads this section she should feel like she could now evaluate any carrier herself.
  • 01
    IHDI hip-healthy certification The International Hip Dysplasia Institute certifies carriers that support the "M" position — knees higher than hips, fabric from knee to knee. Not all carriers have this. Check before you buy.
  • 02
    Newborn-ready without an insert Infant inserts are sold separately, easy to lose, and one more thing to wrestle with at 3am. Carriers that fit a newborn out of the box are worth paying more for.
  • 03
    Weight range that covers the full first year Many budget carriers max out at 15 lbs. That's 3-4 months. A carrier that grows to 33+ lbs is a different value proposition entirely.
  • 04
    Lower back support for the wearer Maya's body matters too. A carrier that transfers weight to the hips rather than the shoulders is the difference between comfortable and done by noon.
