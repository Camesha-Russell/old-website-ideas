If Camesha has specified a pillar, product type, or content type she wants to focus on, honor that. Otherwise surface the highest-value opportunities across the whole site.

## Research process — run these searches

Do not skip the research step. The goal is to find what Maya is actually searching for *right now*, not just what the cluster map already lists. Some of the best posts come from trending products or gaps that weren't on the original plan.

Run all of these searches and synthesize the results:

**Search 1: Current trending baby products**
Search: `trending baby products [current month and year]`
Look for: new product launches, viral baby gear, what's dominating mom group discussions

**Search 2: Seasonal-specific trends**
Search: `best [seasonal category] baby products [current year]` (e.g., "best spring outdoor baby gear 2026")
Look for: what's ranking on page one and who's ranking there — competitor strength signals

**Search 3: High-intent comparison queries**
Search: `[top baby brand] vs [competitor] which is better [current year]`
For 2-3 of the most commercially relevant comparisons in the cluster map
Look for: who's ranking, whether the competition is beatable for a new site

**Search 4: Gap opportunities**
Search: `baby product [category] honest review not worth it 2026`
Look for: products that have a lot of search volume but thin or biased coverage — your "Skip This" post opportunities

**Search 5: Recall and safety news**
Search: `baby product recall [current month year]` and `baby gear safety warning [current year]`
Look for: any recall on products the site has recommended or plans to recommend — these become urgent Safety posts

After running searches, identify the 5-8 strongest post opportunities. Then narrow to the top 3-5 based on the scoring criteria below.

## How to score and rank opportunities

Score each opportunity across four factors. Highest total score = highest priority.

**Seasonal urgency (0-3 points)**
3 = post is directly tied to the current seasonal window (writing this now means it earns traffic at peak)
2 = post is relevant to an upcoming seasonal window within 6 weeks
1 = evergreen content, no seasonal advantage
0 = off-season content

**Commercial value (0-3 points)**
3 = comparison post with high-ticket products (carrier, stroller, monitor — $100+) and a direct affiliate program paying 8%+
2 = best-of page with multiple affiliate opportunities, or comparison post with medium-ticket products
1 = problem-solution or educational post with indirect commercial value
0 = pure trust/authority content with no affiliate revenue this year

**Keyword winnability for a new site (0-3 points)**
3 = top results are mid-size blogs, not mega-authority sites (Wirecutter, BabyGearLab, The Bump) — beatable
2 = mix of authority sites and smaller competitors — possible with strong content
1 = dominated by major authority sites — possible long-term, not a quick win
0 = impossible to rank for in year one regardless of content quality

**Content gap / differentiation (0-3 points)**
3 = existing posts are thin, generic, or clearly haven't been tested — itsmomapproved.com's honest + researched voice is a real differentiator here
2 = decent existing coverage but nothing with the specific angle we'd bring
1 = well-covered topic, would need a very specific angle to stand out
0 = saturated with high-quality content, no clear gap

Maximum score: 12. Recommend the top 3-5 by score.
