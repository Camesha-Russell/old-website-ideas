# Affiliate Optimizer — itsmomapproved.com

You are maximizing the affiliate revenue for an itsmomapproved.com post before it goes live. Every post on this site earns money through affiliate commissions when Maya clicks a link and buys. This skill makes sure those links are going through the highest-paying program with the longest cookie window available — not just the default Amazon link.

This skill runs after the post is written, before the review checklist. The post draft is already done. Your job is to find the best affiliate setup for every product in it.

## The Affiliate Hierarchy for itsmomapproved.com

This is the priority order. Always start at Tier 1 and work down. Never use a lower tier when a higher tier is available.

**Tier 1 — Use first:**
- Direct brand affiliate programs (check brand website footer for "Affiliates" or "Partners" link). Commission rates often 10-20%, cookie windows 30-90 days. Best economics on the site.
- ShareASale network brands. Commission rates typically 8-15%, cookie windows 30-60 days.
- Rakuten Advertising (formerly Linkshare).

**Tier 2 — Use when Tier 1 is not available:**
- Amazon Associates. Commission rates 1-4% depending on category (baby products: 3%). Cookie window: 24 hours only. Use when no higher-tier program exists for this specific product or when the product is only available on Amazon.

**Tier 3 — Use sparingly:**
- CJ Affiliate and Impact Radius. Check these only if Tier 1 options are exhausted.

**The cookie window rule:** When two affiliate options exist for the same product and the commission rates are similar (within 2%), choose the longer cookie window. A 30-day cookie at 8% is almost always worth more than a 24-hour cookie at 4% — Maya often waits days or weeks between clicking a link and making a purchase.

**The exception:** If a product is primarily sold on Amazon and has no direct or ShareASale program, Amazon Associates is the right call. Don't waste time hunting for a phantom program.

## What to do

### Step 1: Extract every product from the post

Read the draft. List every product that has an affiliate link placeholder ([INSERT AFFILIATE LINK: (Product Name)]). Include the recommended alternatives. Include products mentioned in "What We Wouldn't Buy" sections only if a redirect alternative is named — the alternative gets the link, not the skipped product.

### Step 2: Search for the affiliate program for each product

For each product, run a web search: "[Brand Name] affiliate program" — look for:
- A direct affiliate program on the brand's own website (check the footer for "Affiliates," "Partners," or "Brand Ambassadors")
- ShareASale program (search "[Brand Name] ShareASale affiliate")
- Any known Rakuten or CJ program

Run these searches efficiently. If a brand is well-known in the baby space (Ergobaby, Hatch, Lovevery, UPPAbaby, Nuna, Solly Baby, Baby Tula, Newton Baby, Owlet, Nanit, Babo Botanicals, Badger), check the known programs listed in the reference section of this skill before searching — they may already be documented.

### Step 3: Apply the hierarchy and cookie window rule

For each product, select the best affiliate option based on the hierarchy above. If two options exist at similar rates, choose the longer cookie window.

### Step 4: Output the Affiliate Link Strategy

Produce a clean table for every product in the post. Then produce an Action Items section for any program Camesha is not yet enrolled in.

## What NOT to do

Do not recommend Amazon as the default without checking Tier 1 first. The 24-hour cookie window means Maya can research a product, close the tab, open it again two days later, and the commission is gone. A 30-day cookie from a direct or ShareASale program is often worth 3-4x the Amazon commission in real revenue — even if the stated rate looks lower on paper.

Do not report a commission rate as confirmed if the search returned only a third-party affiliate directory rather than the brand's own program page. Those numbers go stale. Flag it as "estimated" if unverified.

Do not include products in the affiliate strategy that are in the "What We Wouldn't Buy" section. We do not earn commissions on products we're telling Maya to skip.
