# Pinterest — itsmomapproved.com

Take a completed post and produce everything needed to publish it on Pinterest. The output is three fully ready pins — each targeting a different search intent — plus board recommendations, hashtags, and a refresh reminder.

Run this after `/seo-optimize` and before publishing. Pinterest is the primary traffic channel for this site in year one. Three pins per post is the minimum. Treat this as a required deliverable, not an afterthought.

---

## Pinterest is a search engine, not a social network

This distinction shapes everything. Maya is not scrolling Pinterest the way she scrolls Instagram. She is searching. She types "Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+" or "what do I actually need on my baby registry" into the Pinterest search bar and she is looking for a clear, specific answer. She saves things she plans to come back to.

This means:
- Keywords in pin titles and descriptions are not optional — they are how she finds the pin
- The text on the image itself (overlay copy) is the first thing she sees before she decides to click
- Saves and repins matter more than likes — they tell Pinterest this pin has shelf life
- A well-optimized pin keeps driving traffic for months or years, unlike a social post that dies in 48 hours

Write every pin as if it is answering a search query Maya is going to type tomorrow.

---

## The three-pin structure

Every post generates three pins. Each pin targets a different type of searcher. Do not create three versions of the same hook.

**Pin 1: The Verdict Pin**
For the Maya who is close to a decision. She already knows roughly what she wants — she just needs someone to close it. This pin leads with the recommendation directly.
- Keyword strategy: exact match to the primary search query ("Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+")
- Hook: the answer, stated confidently
- Tone: Skinny Confidential — punchy, direct, verdict-first
- Example overlay: "Hatch Rest or Rest+? Here's the honest call."
- Example description: "The Hatch Rest does the job. The Rest+ adds a battery and monitor for $20 more. We break down exactly who needs the upgrade and who doesn't. #HatchRest #BabySleep #WhiteNoiseMachine"

**Pin 2: The Research Pin**
For the Maya who is still in research mode. She's not ready to decide yet — she wants to understand her options before committing.
- Keyword strategy: secondary keyword or comparison angle ("best white noise machine for newborns" or "Hatch Rest review")
- Hook: the research signal — shows her that real homework was done
- Tone: warm authority — specific, credible, not salesy
- Example overlay: "We analyzed 30,000 reviews so you don't have to."
- Example description: "The most-asked question in every new mom group: is the Hatch Rest worth it? We dug into the reviews, the specs, and the honest complaints. Here's what we found. #BabyGear #NewbornEssentials #HatchRest"

**Pin 3: The Problem Pin**
For the Maya who doesn't even know this product exists yet — she's searching the pain point, not the product. This is the top-of-funnel pin that brings in cold traffic.
- Keyword strategy: problem-based query ("baby won't sleep," "newborn sleep solution," "best sleep for newborn 2026")
- Hook: the problem first, then the solution
- Tone: Dr. Jazmine register — warm, meets her where she is before giving the answer
- Example overlay: "If your baby only sleeps with white noise, read this."
- Example description: "Sleep deprivation is real. If your baby wakes up every time the house gets quiet, a white noise machine might be the most important thing you buy this month. Here's what we recommend (and what to skip). #NewbornSleep #BabySleepTips #TiredMom"

## How to write each pin

### Pin title
Maximum 100 characters. Keyword in the first 3-4 words. Reads like a real person wrote it — not a keyword list. Follows all brand hard rules: no em dashes, no "actually," no "finally," no question openers.

Good: `Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+: The $20 Difference Explained`
Bad: `Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+ — Which Is Better? Find Out Here!`

### Pin description
Maximum 500 characters (aim for 200-300 — enough to be keyword-rich, short enough to read). Structure: one sentence that restates the hook, two to three sentences with specific useful detail, a soft CTA, hashtags at the end.

The description should sound like itsmomapproved.com's voice — direct, specific, warm. Not a keyword soup sentence. Not a brand tagline. Something Maya would actually read.

### Text overlay copy
This is the text that appears on the image itself. It must:
- Work at thumbnail size — short, large enough to read when the pin is small
- Deliver the hook in 6-12 words
- Be specific, not vague ("We analyzed 30,000 reviews" not "Great product for babies")
- Create enough curiosity or utility that Maya taps to read more

### Hashtags
5-8 per pin. Mix of broad (#BabyGear, #NewbornEssentials) and specific (#HatchRest, #WhiteNoiseMachine). Put them at the end of the description. Do not hashtag every word — that reads as spam.

## Board recommendations

Every pin should be saved to the most relevant board on itsmomapproved.com's Pinterest account. Suggest the primary board for each pin based on content type.

**Recommended board structure for itsmomapproved.com:**
- Baby Sleep Essentials (Sleep pillar content)
- Best Baby Gear (general product recommendations)
- Baby Feeding Must-Haves (Feeding pillar)
- Baby Carriers and Strollers (Carriers and Strollers pillar)
- Baby Play and Development (Play and Development pillar)
- For Moms (For Moms pillar)
- Baby Safety and Recalls (Safety pillar)
- Baby Registry Must-Haves (Registry Reality posts, gift guides)
- Baby Products to Skip (Skip This posts — this board builds serious trust and gets saves from skeptical moms)

If a new board should be created for a specific post, flag it with the suggested board name and a one-line description of what it would contain.

## Seasonal urgency flag

Check the post topic against the seasonal content calendar:
- March-April: Spring outdoor gear (strollers, carriers, outdoor play) — flag as [HIGH PRIORITY NOW]
- May-June: Registry season (best-of lists, registry reality posts) — flag as [HIGH PRIORITY NOW]
- July-August: Travel season — flag as [HIGH PRIORITY NOW]
- November-December: Gift guides — flag as [CRITICAL — must pin heavily in October/November]
- All other months: standard priority

If the post is seasonally timely, say so explicitly and recommend pinning all three pins within the same week rather than spacing them out.

---

## Refresh reminder

Pinterest rewards fresh content. Every pin should be re-created with a new image design every 4-6 months while linking to the same URL. This is not optional — it is how Pinterest accounts maintain momentum.

At the bottom of the Pinterest output, include:

**Refresh reminder:** Re-pin this post with new image designs in [month 4-6 from now]. Same URL, new visuals, same descriptions. Add this to your content calendar.

---

## Brand rules that apply to Pinterest

All brand hard rules apply here. Pinterest content is still itsmomapproved.com content.

- No em dashes in pin titles or descriptions. Not once.
- No "actually," "finally," or "it's worth noting."
- No question openers in pin titles.
- Price should be named as an actual number when relevant — "$20 more" not "slightly more expensive."
- Never recommend everything. If a pin is for a comparison post, it should reflect the verdict clearly, not hedge.

## One more thing: the image brief

After the pin copy, add a short image brief — 2-3 sentences describing what the pin image should look like visually. You are not designing it, but you are giving a clear direction so a designer (or Canva template) can execute it fast.

The image brief should specify:
- The primary visual (product photo, lifestyle shot, text-dominant, or split comparison graphic)
- The mood (clean and minimal, warm and homey, bold and direct)
- The text overlay placement (top, center, or bottom)

Example: "Clean white background. Product photo of both Hatch Rest and Rest+ side by side. Bold text overlay at the bottom: 'Hatch Rest or Rest+? Here's the honest call.' Warm but minimal — feels like a real recommendation, not an ad."

This gives Camesha or a future designer everything they need to execute the visual quickly.
