# SEO Optimize — itsmomapproved.com

Take a drafted post and produce everything needed to publish it with strong on-page SEO. The output is a **WordPress-ready SEO Block** — copy-paste fields for Yoast or Rank Math, a keyword audit of the existing draft, and Pinterest headline optimization.

This skill runs after `/product-post` and before `/review-post`.

---

## The site context that shapes every decision

itsmomapproved.com is a **brand-new site with no domain authority**. This changes the keyword strategy entirely. Targeting "best baby monitors" means competing against Wirecutter, BabyGearLab, and What to Expect — sites with millions of backlinks. A new site will not win that fight.

**Year-one keyword strategy: go narrow and win.**

Comparison queries and long-tail keywords are the target. "Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+" is beatable. "Best white noise machine" is not — not yet. Low competition + high purchase intent is the sweet spot. Once the site has 30–50 published posts and earned domain authority, we revisit head terms.

**Pinterest is the primary traffic channel**, not Google. Pin headlines are as important as title tags. The keyword that works on Google works on Pinterest. Treat both with equal rigor.

---

## Step 1: Identify the primary keyword

Read the drafted post and determine what Maya would type into Google or Pinterest to find it.

**For comparison posts:** Target the exact "X vs Y" phrase — "Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+" or "Ergobaby vs Solly Baby Wrap." These are high-intent, lower-competition queries a new site can rank for.

**For pillar pages:** Target "best [category] for [qualifier]" with a narrowing modifier — "best baby carriers for newborns" rather than "best baby carriers." The modifier makes it winnable.

Then **do a web search** to validate. Search the exact keyword phrase and check:

- Who's ranking on page one? If it's all Wirecutter, The Bump, BabyGearLab, and What to Expect, the competition is too high. Find a longer-tail variation and target that instead.
- Is there a "People also ask" box? Those are secondary keyword opportunities.
- Are the top results thin or outdated? That's a gap worth targeting.

A keyword only makes the cut if it meets all three criteria:
1. Clear purchase or comparison intent
2. Winnable for a new site — top results aren't exclusively mega-authority domains
3. Exact match to what Maya would type when she's close to a decision

---

## Step 2: Audit the draft for keyword placement

Check where the primary keyword appears in the draft the user provided:

| Location | Required? |
|---|---|
| First 100 words | Yes |
| Post title (H1) | Yes |
| At least one H2 heading | Yes |
| Body copy (appears naturally, not stuffed) | Yes |

Flag any gaps. If the keyword is missing from the first 100 words, suggest the specific sentence that should be revised. Don't suggest adding it awkwardly — identify a sentence where it fits naturally.

---

## Step 3: Identify secondary keywords

From the web search results, identify 3–5 related terms that belong naturally in a thorough post on this topic. These are not terms to force in — they are terms that a well-written post would naturally include and that help Google understand the full context.

Example: For a post on Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+, secondary keywords might include "sleep training toddler light," "Hatch Rest app features," "white noise machine for newborns," "Hatch Rest review 2026."

---

## Step 4: Check for keyword cannibalization

Briefly reference the Content Cluster Map. Is there another planned or live post that targets the same or a very similar primary keyword? If yes, flag it. Two posts competing for the same keyword split the site's own authority and both rank lower as a result. One of the two should be adjusted to target a different angle.

## Step 5: Write the SEO Block

Produce the SEO Block using this exact format. Every field must be completed — no placeholders, no "TBD."

---

### SEO BLOCK — [Post Title]

**Primary keyword:** [exact phrase]

**Keyword difficulty for a new site:** [Low / Medium / High] — [one sentence explaining why]

---

**WordPress fields — copy directly into Yoast or Rank Math:**

**SEO Title:** [50–60 characters. Keyword near the front. Reads like a real person wrote it. Sounds like itsmomapproved.com — direct, no fluff, no clickbait. No em dashes. No "actually," "finally," or question openers.]

**Meta description:** [150–160 characters. Include the primary keyword. Give Maya one specific reason to click. Should sound like the brand — honest, direct, a little warm. Not a keyword string. Not "Looking for X? We've got you."]

**URL slug:** [lowercase, hyphens only, no stop words, keyword-first, 4–6 words maximum]

**Focus keyword (Yoast/Rank Math field):** [primary keyword, exact phrase]

**Heading audit:**

Review each heading in the draft. Only suggest changes where they improve keyword placement AND clarity — never force the keyword into a heading unnaturally.

- H1: [current title] — [✅ good as is / suggested revision + reason]
- Key H2s: [flag any that are missing a natural keyword opportunity]

**Secondary keywords — use these naturally in the post:**
1. [keyword]
2. [keyword]
3. [keyword]
4. [keyword]

---

**Image alt text:**

Write alt text for the hero image and one product image. Descriptive first, keyword-enhanced second. Never "image of" — just describe what's in it.

- Hero image: [alt text]

---

**Featured snippet opportunity:**

Comparison posts frequently win featured snippets when the post contains a clear, direct answer paragraph early on. Check whether the drafted post has a paragraph that directly answers "[X] vs [Y]: which is better?" in 2–3 sentences. If yes, flag it as the featured snippet candidate. If no, suggest where a short direct-answer paragraph could be inserted without disrupting the flow.

---

**Internal links:**
- Pillar page link: ✅ / ❌ (if ❌, this is required before publishing — flag it)
- Pillar page: [title]
- Other internal linking opportunities: [any other live or in-progress posts this post could naturally reference]

---

**Pinterest SEO:**

Three pin headlines, fully optimized for Pinterest search. Pinterest is the primary traffic channel — these are not an afterthought. Each pin targets a distinct search angle.

- **Pin 1:** [Targets the primary keyword — exact match or very close. Benefit-forward. Specific.]
- **Pin 2:** [Targets a secondary keyword or different angle. Different hook from Pin 1.]
- **Pin 3:** [Question format or comparison format — targets a "People also ask" style query.]

---

**Keyword audit of existing draft:**

- First 100 words: ✅ / ❌
- Post title (H1): ✅ / ❌
- At least one H2: ✅ / ❌
- Natural frequency in body: ✅ / ❌ (note if sparse or stuffed)

For any ❌, identify the specific fix — the exact sentence or heading to revise.

## Voice rules for SEO copy

The SEO title and meta description are the first thing Maya sees on a search results page. They represent the brand. Apply the same hard rules here as in the posts:

- No em dashes (—). Not in the title tag, not in the meta description. Restructure if needed.
- No "actually," "finally," or "it's worth noting."
- No question openers — "Looking for the best white noise machine?" is not how itsmomapproved.com speaks.
- The meta description should read like Maya's trusted friend gave a one-sentence answer. Not like a keyword compliance exercise.

**Bad meta description:**
*"Looking for a Hatch Rest vs Hatch Rest+ comparison? We review both to help you decide which white noise machine is best for your baby."*

**Good meta description:**
*"The Hatch Rest works from newborn through toddlerhood. The Rest+ adds a few extras. Here's whether the upgrade is worth the $30 difference."*

The good version sounds like someone actually answered the question. The bad version sounds like it was written for a crawler.

---

## Delivery format

Present the complete SEO Block as a clean section the user can reference while setting up the post in WordPress. Then add a brief "Draft Adjustments" note listing any specific changes recommended to the existing post draft (keyword gaps, heading revisions, featured snippet paragraph). Keep the adjustments note tight — the user is going to act on it, not read an essay.

If the keyword chosen needs to change from what the post was drafted around, explain why clearly and concisely before presenting the block.
